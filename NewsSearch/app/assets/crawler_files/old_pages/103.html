Protest probe: top cop speaks

Tuesday, December 16, 2014

Commissioner of Police Andy Tsang Wai-hung, conspicuously silent during 79 days of protests, returned to the public eye to say a probe into Occupy Central will be completed within three months.

More arrests are likely, in addition to the 955 already arrested, Tsang said yesterday.

Giving his first press conference since the clearance of the last protest zone in Causeway Bay, he said 130 police officers had been injured and 221 activists given medical attention.

"Police will endeavor to complete the investigation as soon as possible, including arresting other offenders particularly the principal instigators," Tsang went on.

"The difficulties and complexities of this operation were unprecedented.

"The duration of the illegal occupations, the vast numbers of protesters involved, mobilization capabilities of the organizers, as well as the methods used and the radical acts undertaken by protesters, could not be anticipated.

"It started with violence but ended in a peaceful way. Whether if it was a success or failure, it is something citizens should think about."

He again defended the use of tear gas on September 28, saying it minimized the potential harm to police and activists.

There have been 1,972 complaints made against police during the past 11 weeks, with 137 by those directly affected.

"We will investigate in an impartial and fair way ... and report to the Independent Police Complaints Council every month," Tsang said.

A police sergeant who collapsed during the clearance of Admiralty last week was in a critical condition in Ruttonjee Hospital.

KEVIN CHENG and KENNETH LAU
