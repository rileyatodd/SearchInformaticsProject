Obama to military: Take pride in your accomplishments

President Obama Monday praised troops for their service in Afghanistan and told them to take pride in the accomplishments as the United States' combat mission there comes to an end.

Obama marked the end of the war in Afghanistan during a rousing address to thousand of military members and their families in an aircraft hangar at the Joint Base McGuire-Dix-Lakenurst. The base has played a key role in deployments to Afghanistan.

"Our nation is marking an important milestone," Obama told the crowd. "Nobody in history has been able to do what you have done."

U.S. and NATO troops closed their operational command in Afghanistan last week after 13 years of war. Some troops will remain, however, to carry out operations against Taliban and al Qaeda targets.

"On behalf of a grateful nation I want to say welcome home. We're glad to have you back," Obama said. "We're free and safe and secure over here because you are willing to serve over there. That's the selfless character of our military."

The President's visit comes at a time when units at the base have been transporting supplies to Liberia during the Ebola crisis and aerially refueling U.S. warplanes that have struck at the heart of ISIS, the Islamic State in Iraq and Syria.

Obama was joined by Gov. Christie and U.S. Sen. Robert Menendez (D., N.J.), and other members of the New Jersey congressional delegation.

The President quipped that "they were already lobbying me about the base on the way in."

The base is home to several KC-10 refueling aircraft. Air Force and Defense Department officials have signaled the Obama administration's intention to phase out the planes.

More than half of them are stationed at the Joint Base and been used to refuel U.S. planes attacking ISIS.

"Elimination of these 32 aircraft would decimate the installation's crucial refueling mission and the valuable human capital that supports it," wrote US Rep. Chris Smith to Obama in a Dec. 12 letter. ". . . accordingly I urge you to again reject this misguided proposal."

The visit also comes as the state works on strategies for inoculating the Joint Base and other military installations from being closed by the Defense Department.

Lt. Gov. Kim Guadagno chairs the New Jersey Military Installation Growth and Development Task Force, which includes Brig. Gen. Michael L. Cunniff, adjutant general for the New Jersey Department of Military and Veterans Affairs, and former U.S. Rep. Jim Saxton.

Saxton has said that installations such as the Joint Base are likely to face new closure threats as the nation cuts back on military spending.

"I am encouraged by President Obama's recognition of these service members, their families, all of their sacrifices and their importance to this country," said Sen. Diane Allen (R., Burlington County). "This one-of-a-kind military base is truly the lifeblood of our region, as it keeps us safe and protects our freedom by air, land and sea.

"I hope the President's visit is a sign that he will join me in doing whatever is needed to preserve the Joint Base and enhance its operations as our military continues to evolve."

Earlier this year, Allen introduced a state budget resolution requesting an appropriation for the preservation of the base, as the joint base is vulnerable to being realigned or closed by the federal Base Realignment and Closure Commission (BRAC).

Following Allen's request, a $200,000 appropriation for the preservation of the base was included in the current fiscal budget signed by Christie. The appropriation for the Council on Armed Forces and Veterans' Affairs to protect McGuire-Dix-Lakehurst from closure would be made statute and would be considered for inclusion in each coming budget.

The sprawling 60-square-mile Joint Base in Burlington and Ocean Counties provides about 40,000 military and civilian jobs and contributes $7 billion each year to the state economy, according to a Rutgers University study. It also supports 65,000 off-base jobs. About $118.7 million in annual state tax revenues are generated from the operation of the base.

The Associated Press contributed to this report.
