Feds inaugurate new Texas family detention center near San Antonio

WILL WEISSERT
Published: 15 December 2014 01:46 PM
Updated: 15 December 2014 01:46 PM

DILLEY, Texas — A former camp for oil field workers in rural South Texas will become a new family detention center amid the recent influx of children crossing the U.S.-Mexico border.

The first of up to 480 immigrants are expected to arrive this week in Dilley, about 70 miles southwest of San Antonio. Temporary housing nearby under construction could hold an additional 2,400 immigrants.

They will be held in cottages that can serve up to 8 people each.

The facility to be inaugurated Monday opens as officials close a temporary family immigration detention center on the grounds of a law enforcement training center in New Mexico.

While border crossings of children have slowed considerably from the summer, advocates say immigrant families are often fleeing drug violence or extreme poverty in Central America.
