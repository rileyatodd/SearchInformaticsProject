Survey: Conditions decline for N.Y. manufacturers

By ANDREA DECKERT
Rochester Business Journal
December 15, 2014

Business activity in December declined for New York manufacturers, the Federal Reserve Bank of New York’s Empire State Manufacturing Survey shows.

For the first time in nearly two years, the general business conditions index signaled a decline in business activity for New York manufacturers. The index retreated 14 points to minus 3.6 in December, with 19 percent of respondents reporting conditions had improved over the month and 23 percent reporting conditions had worsened.

Overall, readings for the headline index during the fourth quarter mark a downshift in activity from the levels seen from May through September, the survey shows.

The new orders index fell 11 points to minus 2, indicating a small decline in orders, and the shipments index dropped 12 points to minus 0.2. The unfilled orders index plummeted 17 points to minus 24. The delivery time index drifted down to minus 14.6, suggesting delivery times were shorter, and the inventories index fell to minu 11.5, pointing to lower inventory levels.

The prices paid index was little changed at 10.4, signaling a modest increase in input prices for a third consecutive month. After falling to zero in November, the prices received index advanced six points to 6.3 in December.

Labor market indicators were mixed. The index for number of employees, at 8.3, held steady for a third consecutive month, suggesting employment levels continued to increase. The average workweek index, by contrast, declined for a fourth consecutive month, and at minus 11.5, pointed to a noteworthy decline in hours worked.

Indexes assessing the six-month outlook were generally lower this month, but conveyed optimism about future business activity.

The index for future general business conditions fell nine points to 38.6, but was still a fairly high figure by historical standards, officials said. The index for expected number of employees was 20.8, indicating employment is expected to expand briskly, and the future average workweek index climbed to 12.5, its highest level in more than two years.

The capital expenditures index fell 12 points to 15.6, and the technology spending index inched down to 17.7.
