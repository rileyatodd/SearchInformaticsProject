1st LD Writethru: U.S. dollar rises amid mixed data

Tweet
Enlarge
(Globalpost/GlobalPost)

NEW YORK, Dec. 15 (Xinhua) -- The U.S. dollar climbed against most major currencies on Monday as economic data from the country came out mixed.

The Federal Reserve reported on Monday that U.S. industrial production for November jumped 1.3 percent, beating earlier consensus of a 0.7 percent gain.

However, manufacturing activity in the New York region declined in December, said the Federal Reserve Bank of New York on Monday. According to the latest Empire State Manufacturing Survey, the headline general business conditions index dropped 14 points to minus 3.6, its first negative reading in nearly two years.

Following a four-point uptick last month, U.S. builder confidence in the market for newly built single-family homes fell one point in December to a level of 57 on the National Association of Home Builders/Wells Fargo Housing Market Index (HMI). Though the latest reading was below market expectation, it remains above breakeven 50.

Investors were awaiting the closely-watched Fed December meeting scheduled for Tuesday and Wednesday to get a clearer view of when the central bank would raise interest rates.

The Japanese yen rose against the U.S. dollar as losses in global equity markets and crude prices boosted demand for safe- haven assets.

In late New York trading, the euro decreased to 1.2435 dollars from 1.2451 dollars in the previous session, and the British pound declined to 1.5642 dollars from 1.5706 dollars. The Australian dollar went down to 0.8222 dollar from 0.8250 dollar.

The U.S. dollar bought 117.71 Japanese yen, lower than 118.75 yen of the previous session. The U.S. dollar went up to 0.9658 Swiss francs from 0.9645 Swiss francs, and it moved up to 1.1648 Canadian dollars from 1.1568 Canadian dollars.
