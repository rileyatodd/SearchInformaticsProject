Should Case Keenum or Thad Lewis start for the Texans?

Case Keenum is returning to the Texans.

Keenum, the former record-breaking University of Houston quarterback who was on St. Louis’ practice squad, will be signed and added to the Texans’ active roster for the last two games against Baltimore and Jacksonville at NRG Stadium.

Because Ryan Fitzpatrick (leg) and Tom Savage (knee) were injured in Sunday’s 17-10 loss at Indianapolis, the Texans are in the market for at least one quarterback and possibly two to join recently signed free-agent Thad Lewis.

Fitzpatrick’s season is over with the broken leg. If Savage suffered extensive damage to his knee, as many suspect, Lewis and Keenum should be active against the Ravens. Savage is going to have an MRI Monday to determine the extent to his injury.

Lewis was signed on Nov. 24 after Ryan Mallett was lost for the season with a torn pectoral muscle.

The Texans placed Keenum on waivers Aug. 31 to make room for Mallett, who was acquired from New England for a conditional seventh-round draft choice in 2016.

Keenum spent the offseason and preseason working with coach Bill O’Brien and quarterbacks coach George Godsey, so he has experience in the Texans’ system.

Keenum, who signed with the Texans as an undrafted free agent in 2012, started eight games last season. He finished with nine touchdowns, 1,760 yards, six interceptions and a 78.2 rating.

Fitzpatrick started the first nine games of this season but was benched for Mallett for two weeks. After Mallett’s injury, Fitzpatrick returned to his starting role. He started the last three games, including Sunday, when he suffered a broken leg.

Savage has been Fitzpatrick’s backup recently. He was injured on the Texans’ final series on Sunday. He played the rest of the game. Savage, who said he was OK, was noticeably limping in the locker room after the game.

The Texans, who are 7-7 and not yet eliminated from the playoffs, host the Ravens on Sunday. Former Texans head coach Gary Kubiak is the Ravens’ offensive coordinator.
