Case Keenum or Thad Lewis will start for Texans on Sunday

Case Keenum or Thad Lewis. That’s the decision that Bill O’Brien will make this week.

The Texans coach said the team has brought back Keenum, the former UH star quarterback, and he will compete against Lewis, signed last month as a free-agent, for the starting position on Sunday against the Ravens.

“We’ll divide the reps (for QBs) as we go through the week,” O’Brien said. “We’ll do a good job of taking a good look at both of them.”

O’Brien also said that rookie Tom Savage will not be available against the Ravens and could be out longer. Savage suffered a knee injury on the final series in Sunday’s loss at the Colts.

Also on Sunday, starting quarterback Ryan Fitzpatrick suffered a broken leg.

Keenum has spent more time than Lewis with O’Brien, having practiced with him during training camp before he was cut in late August. Lewis was signed last month, when quarterback Ryan Mallett was lost to a season-ending injury.

“There’s a definite advantage with (Keenum),” O’Brien said. “He was here when we installed the offense. He spent a lot of time with us.

“He’d been in our system not long ago. A lot of the things we’re doing are what we were doing. He’s a bright guy.”

Fitzpatrick, Mallett and Savage give the Texans three quarterbacks to miss games this season. O’Brien said he’s never seen a quarterback situation like the Texans.

“But, that’s the NFL,” he said. “We’re looking forward to the challenge.”

O’Brien said the Texans are unlikely to sign a third quarterback for the 53-man roster. They are likely to sign a practice squad quarterback to run the scout team.
