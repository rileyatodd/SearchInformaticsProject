How the top 100 restaurants in Houston for 2014 were picked

By Alison Cook

As I mention each year at the dawn of a new Top 100 Restaurants list, these are not necessarily Houston's fanciest restaurants, nor the most expensive, nor the ones most worthy of multiple stars in some fading fine-dining universe where napery and carpeting and luxury ingredients mattered intensely.

1: Oxheart

By Alison Cook

Once again, this small and informal tasting-menu restaurant in the Warehouse District deserves to be known as Houston's best.

2: The Pass

By Alison Cook

From the moment the festive champagne cart rolls up to your table in this calm, chaste dining room, The Pass delivers a type of modern luxury that is rare in Houston.

3: Killen's Barbecue

By Alison Cook

The constant lines outside this exciting Pearland destination attest to the grandeur of chef Ronnie Killen's meticulously smoked meats.

By Alison Cook

Chef-owner Chris Kinjo's sushi mecca bounced back from a disastrous 2013 kitchen fire handsomer and better staffed than before.

11: Hugo's

By Alison Cook

After 12 years, chef Hugo Ortega's beautifully run regional Mexican restaurant qualifies as a Houston cultural treasure.

12: Pax Americana

By Alison Cook

This fledgling restaurant is one to watch. Co-chefs Adam Dorris and Plinio Sandalio — the former a specialist in savory dishes, the latter in sweet — collaborate on a smart short menu devoted to painstakingly curated American ingredients, particularly from the Gulf Coast.

13: Himalaya

By Alison Cook

15: Mala Sichuan Bistro

By Alison Cook

Continue Reading

16: Triniti

By Alison Cook

New initiatives are keeping this gorgeous contemporary restaurant relevant to the city's dining conversation.

Continue Reading

17: Kata Robata

By Alison Cook

From Copper River sockeye in season to the briny joys of wild Japanese horse mackerel, the sushi and sashimi crafted by chef Manabu Horiuchi is as immaculate as always at this crowded Upper Kirby haunt.

18: Reef

By Alison Cook

It's fun to see new ideas percolating again at this bustling Midtown Gulf seafood mecca, which had been running in place since its groundbreaking debut seven years ago when chef Bryan Caswell introduced lesser-known species and bycatch to Houston.

Continue Reading

19: Roost

By Alison Cook

This unassuming little Montrose bistro may look like a midcentury basement rec room, but it just gets better — and more expressive of the blending of international cuisines to which Houstonians are heir.

20: Pho Binh Trailer

What is there to say about this beloved southeast Houston landmark other than that the family-run soup kitchen is essential to our civic life?

21: Dolce Vita Pizzeria Enoteca

Continue Reading

22: Killen's Steakhouse

By Alison Cook

Chef Ronnie Killen may be applying scholarly attention to his new barbecue spot of late, but his splendid Texas steakhouse — still under his watchful eye — clicks right along in the capable hands of DeeDee Killen and chef de cuisine Teddy Lopez.

23: Good Dog Houston

By Alison Cook

Once upon a time it was a food truck. Now Amalia Pferd and Daniel Caballero's new brick-and-mortar restaurant — don't anybody dare call it "just a hot-dog joint" — is an object lesson in the ways the city's burgeoning food-truck scene has enriched our dining options.

24: Hubcap Grill

Burger maestro Ricky Craig has expanded to Kemah this year without missing a beat in quality.
