The Pirate Bay is back online - just a week after being taken down by Swedish police

  • Dec 15, 2014 11:07
  • By Olivia Solon

The Pirate Bay has reappeared in another form after torrent site Isohunt copied its database

  • Share
  • Share
  • Tweet
  • +1
  • Email

The Pirate Bay has re-emerged less than a week after it was pulled down by Swedish police.

The popular peer-to-peer filesharing site - which offers search tool for finding and downloading copies of movies and music - was taken down after Swedish police targeted a server room in Stockholm in connection with copyright violations.

But the site has now been salvaged by torrent site Isohunt, which claims to have copied The Pirate Bay's database and housed it on a new site Oldpiratebay.org.

Isohunt says it's saved the Pirate Bay for posterity purposes "for generations of users".

"It’ll be missed. It’ll be remembered as the pilgrim of freedom and possibilities on the web. It’s a symbol of liberty for a generation of internet users," says the new site.

While it sounds like the new Pirate Bay site is just an archive, it's actually serving up new content.

IsoHunt says that if The Pirate Bay recovers, it will take the site down.

Anyone interested in the new service should be wary about any requests being made for money to support the site - some opportunistic scammers have been asking for donations using a Twitter account called @oldpiratebay.
