Anonymous targets Sweden over Pirate Bay

HANNAH FRANCIS
Last updated 10:04, December 16 2014
Screenshot: oldpiratebay.org

Rival torrent-sharing site Isohunt has launched a lookalike site following the closure of The Pirate Bay.

A "hacktivist" group using the Anonymous banner claims it has leaked official login details of Swedish government email accounts as payback for last week's raid that shut down The Pirate Bay.

"HagashTeam" released the sensitive data in a document on Pastebin, which also includes login credentials for what appear to be official email accounts of the Israeli, Indian, Brazilian, Argentinian and Mexican administrations.

The hackers said the leak was "in retaliation" for the Pirate Bay raid.

BREAKING: Emails from Swedish government were hacked in retaliation for the seizure of servers of The Pirate Bay http://t.co/UaYiyQq5W9

— Anonymous (@AnonymousGlobo) December 14, 2014

On Tuesday, Swedish authorities seized The Pirate Bay's servers and computers from a data centre in Greater Stockholm "because of a copyright infringement", a police spokesman said.

The Pirate Bay is one of the largest and most well-known torrent-sharing websites, which allow users to share content for free - including copyrighted films and music - by uploading and downloading files directly from each other.

Targeted governments could not confirm to Fairfax Media at the time of writing whether the leaked login credentials were genuine.

Target: http://t.co/7n6zorlkFH IP: 147.186.254.52 #Anonymous #OpPirateDay #LastRound #DDOS #Retweet @YourAnonNews @AnonymousGlobo

— AnonMafiaCyberFamily (@AnonMafiaFamily) December 14, 2014

Another group under the Anonymous banner has tweeted the URL and IP address purportedly linked to the Swedish police force and is now calling for hackers to target the website.

Anonymous is a decentralised, loosely affiliated network of hackers that targets institutions of power and promotes transparency and freedom. It has defended The Pirate Bay previously.

An English language Swedish news outlet, The Local, reported that Anonymous hacked Swedish internet company Telia on Friday, causing disruption to customers' internet connections.

Torrent Freak has noted a boost to traffic at rival torrenting sites such as KickassTorrents as a result of the shutdown.

A number of proxy or "mirror" websites, including thepiratebay.cr, thepiratebay.hk and thepiratebay.org.es, are functioning, however Torrent Freak said users can't upload new content.

Rival Isohunt launched a copycat site at oldpiratebay.org which does allow users to upload files.

In a "postmortem", Isohunt claimed it "copied the database of Pirate Bay in order to save it for generations of users". However, Torrent Freak reported that its torrent archive was smaller than that of the original Pirate Bay.

The news outlet said these sites were not affiliated with the original Pirate Bay site and warned torrenters to be wary of scams.

Swedish courts have fined and jailed The Pirate Bay's four co-founders Carl Lundstrom, Fredrik Neij, Gottfrid Svartholm Warg and Peter Sunde.

Sunde was recently released from jail after five months.

In a blog post he distanced himself from the site, suggesting the people who ran The Pirate Bay today had no soul.

He said it felt good to see the site shut down, and mused that it could be gone forever.

 - Fairfax Media Australia
