River Oaks home hosts annual Holiday Schmooze

Posted on December 11, 2014 | By Amber Elliott
  • Print
  • —

The annual Holiday Schmooze fundraiser caused somewhat of a traffic jam in River Oaks on Wednesday night. But even the subsequent road closures didn’t deter 200 merrymakers from flooding the twinkling-light-filled neighborhood to “party with a purpose” in support of Legacy Community Health Services’ AIDS and HIV programs.

Once inside Kathryn and Jeff Smith’s illuminated home, schmoozing was in full swing. The hostess – who matched her daughter Mary Kate Smith in festive emerald green and black taffeta ensembles – welcomed guests upon arrival before inviting them to pose Christmas-card style in front of an ornament-lined staircase.

Under Jackson Hicks’ direction, elegant silver trays of empanadas, tuna tartare and mini beef wellingtons floated throughout the crowd. In the formal dining room, shrimp bisque shooters and an impressive cheese selection were offered on gold-rimmed china.

Legacy’s executive director Katy Caldwell paused the mingling briefly to share the origins of Schmooze, which began in 1996 and has helped serve 4,000 individuals living with HIV.

“The food and alcohol and festivities are more important than anything we have to say,” quipped Jeff Smith, with Kathryn by his side. “We’ve been long time supporters of Legacy; they provide an amazing quality of healthcare without the burden of having to pay for it. Lastly, I apologize for the traffic – I had nothing to do with it.”

The cocktail soiree raised just over $200,ooo.

Spreading holiday cheer: Chris Goins, Nick Espinosa, Beatrica Zarco, Michael Mithoff, Christie and Mark Sullivan, Diane Lokey Farb, Amy and Todd Miller, Pat Breen, Chree Boydstun, Joe Oliver, Melanie Gray, June Christensen, Bryan Hvlinka, Tim Surratt, Claire Comier Theilke, and Courtney Hopson.
