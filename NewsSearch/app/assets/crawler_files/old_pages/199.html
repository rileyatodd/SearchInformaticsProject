NFLPA Files Federal Suit in AP Suspension Case

December 15, 2014 Updated Dec 15, 2014 at 3:46 PM CST

Minneapolis, MN (NNCNOW.com) --- The NFL Players' Association has filed a lawsuit in U.S. District Court in Minneapolis seeking to overturn the suspension of Minnesota Vikings running back Adrian Peterson.

The suit alleges that third-party arbitrator Harold Henderson, who upheld the suspension last Friday, was biased in his ruling and acted against the collective bargaining agreement between the league and the players' union.

  • Click here to subscribe to our daily newsletter.

Peterson has been suspended for a minimum of six games by the NFL for his role in what was termed by the league as "an incident of abusive discipline" of his four-year old son.

The former NFL MVP was initially charged with felony injury of a child, but that was reduced to misdemeanor reckless assault as part of a plea deal that had Peterson plead no contest.

One of the big points the union plans to argue is that Peterson is being retroactively punished by the league and that, under the personal conduct policy in place when the incident took place, he should only be subject to a two-game suspension.

Henderson, a former NFL executive appointed as a third-party arbitrator in this case, issued his decision late last Friday afternoon and concluded that Peterson "has not demonstrated that the process and procedures surrounding his discipline were not fair and consistent."

The decision upheld a minimum six-game suspension for Peterson, who would stand to lose nearly $4.2 million in game checks during that absence.
