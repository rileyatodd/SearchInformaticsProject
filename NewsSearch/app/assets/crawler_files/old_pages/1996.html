Join us for our Summer Series

28/11/2014

For the first time in its 18 year history, Australian Story will air selected 2014 episodes weeknights at 6:25pm on ABC TV.

1/12/14 - Driving Miss Davis
The quirky, life affirming story of a young Australian from country Victoria, a car with a history, and a novel that's on its way to becoming a runaway success around the world.


2/12/14 - The Back of His Mind
Three years ago, 35 year old fashion photographer David Murrell was told he had just months to live. But in a lucky coincidence his own father, a cell biologist, emerged as a possible saviour.


3/12/14 - Call Me Cate
Lt Col. Cate McGregor AM has been a participant in some of the most significant chapters in recent Australian history. Now, she is the most high profile individual in Australia to 'come out' as a transgendered person.


4/12/14 - Lock Me Away
At nearly 70 Drew Hutton has become the leader of a campaign that has untied former sworn enemies – farmers and greenies. He reveals the makings of this leadership, the drive that keeps him fighting and the personal toll it has taken.


5/12/14 - An Unnatural History
David Rennie's skills as a self-taught photographer were recognised last year when he won the prestigious Australian Geographic ANZANG award for a dramatic shot showing a young osprey and a spoonbill narrowly avoiding a collision in mid air.


8/12/14 - The House Of Tara
Seven years after Tara Winkler first went to Cambodia, the organisation she created is no longer an orphanage but a community development organisation offering help to poor families and more than 100 children each day.


9/12/14 - Out Of The Box
Adam Boland was still in his twenties when he created a new kind of television breakfast show. It's been a tempestuous ride and now he's walking away from the industry he once loved.


10/12/14 - Set In Stone
Explorer Percy Trezise first came across the ancient Quinkan rock art over fifty years ago. Today, his two sons contemplate their own uncertain future after spending years guiding visitors into the rock art sites and managing their father’s legacy.


11/12/14 - A Complicated Life Part 1
Getting on for a decade after his death, the legendary businessman Kerry Packer remains an intriguing and puzzling figure.


12/12/14 - A Complicated Life Part 2
The conclusion of our story about the legendary businessman Kerry Packer...


15/12/14 - A Very Good Rascal
The bitter sweet personal story behind the hard-living facade of iconic Australian rock musician, Doc Neeson and a look at the history and legacy of the legendary Angels band.


16/12/14 -The Edge of the Mountain
It was supposed to be the adventure of a lifetime but when Australian nurse Christy King led a group of experienced trekkers into the PNG jungle everything changed in an instant.


17/12/14- In Tracy’s Corner
This is the poignant story of the unsolved and savage murder of a woman called Tracy Connelly and the unexpected consequences of her death.


18/12/14 -The Last Commando Part 1
Corporal Baird was killed in action in Afghanistan . Now, for the first time, those closest to him are telling his story...


19/12/14 -The Last Commando Part 2
Corporal Cameron Baird was awarded a posthumous Victoria Cross for his raw courage in the heat of combat. In this conclusion, his commando colleagues provide a poignant and riveting account of those fatal hours...


22/12/14 -The Other Side
Matthew Reilly is one of the most successful authors Australia has ever produced. In his books heroes prevail against impossible odds. But in his own life he's faced unexpected adversity from the most unlikely quarter.


23/12/14 -All the Right Moves Part 1
Part One of the program sheds new light on Kieren Perkins and one of the more memorable careers in Australian sporting history.


24/12/14 -All the Right Moves Part 2
The conclusion to our story on Kieren Perkins who now aged forty, has forged a second career as a successful banking executive and finally come to terms with the strange and difficult process of rocketing from sporting idol to 'nobody'.


25/12/14 -Out Of The Kitchen
Jules Allen provides an intriguing glimpse behind the scenes of the reality TV phenomenon that's captured the TV landscape in Australia.


26/12/14 -Getting The Hang Of It
Australian Story has been 'embedded' with Jacqui Lambie as she learns the ropes in parliament and navigates a series of headline-grabbing dramas.


29/12/14 -The Past Is A Foreign Country Part 1
Jan Russ is well recognised in the entertainment industry for her role in helping to launch the careers of some of Australia's biggest stars but her personal story has all the ingredients of a prime time drama playing out over fifty years.


30/12/14 -The Past Is A Foreign Country Part 2
In this conclusion we find out what happened when Jan tracked down her lost daughter and learn of her son Sam's emergence as the king of comedy on South Korean TV.


31/12/14 -His Driving Force
Col Reynolds started raising funds to help children with cancer after a chance encounter at a hospital. His alliance with scientist Prof. Peter Gunning has resulted in a major breakthrough in the race to cure cancer.


1/1/15 -Forget Me Not
How many people with a diagnosis of dementia join a dating agency? How many more find true love? This is just one of the many intriguing aspects of Christine Bryden's story...


2/1/15 -Talk of the Town
Popular singer songwriter Megan Washington performs on stage for huge audiences. But she's been terrified of public speaking because from the age of five, she's suffered a debilitating stutter.


5/1/15 -The Game Changer
Ten years ago Sean Willmore set up an organisation called The Thin Green Line Foundation to 'protect the protectors' – wildlife rangers who were being killed and maimed. Now he has the ear of world leaders, rock stars and British Royal Princes...


6/1/15 -Leaps And Bounds
The friendship between Li Cunxin and Lady Deborah MacMillan, the Australian-born wife of the famous ballet choreographer Sir Kenneth MacMillan, and the trials and tribulations behind the Queensland Ballet’s staging of Sir Kenneth’s version of 'Romeo and Juliet'.


7/1/15 -Searching for C11 Part 1
At the age of 21, aeronautical engineer Lauren Burns was told a family secret that turned her world upside down.


8/1/15 -Searching for C11 Part 2
Dr Lauren Burns finally finds out the identity of her biological father. To the amazement of all, he turns out to be part of a particularly distinguished Australian family.


9/1/15 -Before The Storm
Joanne McCarthy's exposure of a national scandal emerging in the Newcastle and Hunter Valley region played a large part in the establishment of one of Australia's biggest-ever Royal Commissions.


12/1/15 -Vanishing Act
More than three million viewers watched Karise Eden named as 'The Voice Australia' in 2012. In this moving story the 22 year old singer finally reveals what happened after 'The Voice'.


13/1/15 -The Pursuit Of Happiness
Stand up comedy sensation Fiona O'Loughlin and those closest to her candidly confront the darkest demons of an issue that has famously bedevilled many male comedians, but is less acknowledged by women in the public eye.


14/1/15 -I Am Jack
A return to the story of radio and TV personality Ian 'Dano' Rogerson and his wife Nicole, who gave up their home, careers and an exciting lifestyle for the love of their son, Jack.


15/1/15 – Just Call Me Bob Pt 1
It's been a while since the words popular and politician appeared credibly in the same sentence. But a generation ago one Prime Minister, Bob Hawke, basked in sustained acclaim.


16/1/15 – Just Call Me Bob Pt 2
This moving final episode covers the most dramatic years of all including the Hawke ascendancy in the eighties, loss of the Prime Ministership, family breakdown, re-marriage and reconciliation.


19/1/15 -The Innocent Pt 1
On a summer's day in 1992 a thirteen year old schoolgirl called Prue Bird vanished without a trace. It became one of Victoria's most enduring mysteries...


20/1/15-The Innocent Pt 2
The conclusion to the astonishing family crime saga behind the disappearance of Melbourne teenager Prue Bird.


21/1/15 - How I Met Your Father
Advertising executive Aminah Hart lost two baby boys as a result of a genetic disorder passed from mother to son. Anonymous sperm donation and IVF offered a last chance to have a healthy child.


22/1/15 - Out Of The Shoebox
Christiaan Van Vuuren was diagnosed with drug resistant tuberculosis and forced into hospital quarantine for six months. He made humorous video clips documenting his experiences in the isolation ward which went viral around the world on YouTube.


23/1/15 - Letter From Wooleen
The story of historic 'Wooleen' and the young couple who live there drew a big reaction from viewers three years ago. Since that time, the big challenges of the property have increased for David Pollock and Frances Jones...
