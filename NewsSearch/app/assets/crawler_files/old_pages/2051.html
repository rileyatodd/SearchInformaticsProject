One Plus One

  1. One Plus One: Helen Clark

    As head of the United Nations Development Programme, Helen Clark is considered one of the most powerful women in the world. Speculation is now mounting that Ms Clark, former prime minister of New Zealand, could be in line for the UN’s top job.

  2. One Plus One: John Olsen

    John Olsen is one of Australia’s greatest artists whose life and six-decade career have proved every bit as colourful as his paintings. He shares his memories with Jane Hutcheon.

  3. One Plus One: Pamela Stephenson Connolly

    Pamela Stephenson Connolly is a comedy actor, writer and clinical psychologist whose lifelong love of dance has inspired yet another career move. She speaks with Jane Hutcheon about family, fame and the health of her comedian husband, Billy Connolly.

  4. One Plus One: Molly Meldrum

    Ian “Molly” Meldrum is a legendary figure in the Australian pop music industry but, he tells Jane Hutcheon, there is nothing he hates more than to be called a powerbroker.

  5. One Plus One: Kim Williams

    Kim Williams had his heart set on a career in music, but became of Australia’s most powerful media figures. The former News Corp CEO speaks with Jane Hutcheon about life, loss and leadership.

  6. One Plus One: Jane Turner

    Jane Turner became a household name for her portrayal of the suburban housewife Kath Day-Knight in the comedy series Kath and Kim. But what lies beneath the wig? She opens up to Jane Hutcheon.

  7. One Plus One: Damien Thomlinson

    The final of a three-part series on survivors. Damien Thomlinson was a Special Forces Commando in Afghanistan, when his vehicle drove over a bomb. Today, as a double amputee, he tells Jane Hutcheon that he’s determined to see life as one with challenges, not obstacles.

Lateline

  1. Friday Forum

    Steve Ciobo, Parliamentary Secretary to the Treasurer and Labor's Finance Spokesman, Tony Burke, discuss a turbulent year in politics.

  2. ICAC to appeal judgement to stop investigating Crown Prosecutor

    The nation's most powerful corruption watchdog, ICAC, will appeal the judgment that's shut down its investigation of leading Crown Prosecutor, Margaret Cunneen.

  3. Dust Storm swallows Queensland town

    A huge dust cloud has rolled across outback Queensland, turning day into night at Bedourie in far west Queensland.

  4. Call for targets to reduce indigenous incarceration

    Social Justice Commissioner Mick Gooda has demanded action over the spiralling rate of indigenous incarceration which he calls a 'catastrophe' and a 'human rights crisis'

  5. A turbulent political year

    A look back at the Abbott Government's record in Government.

    The Treasurer Joe Hockey admits he's on notice to improve his performance over summer, saying some ministerial colleagues are 'not happy' about budget cuts he's forced on them.

  7. Shadow Treasurer Chris Bowen

    The alternative Treasurer, Chris Bowen, won't guarantee a Labor Government would return $80 billion worth of proposed cuts to health and education.

  8. U.S. to launch investigation into death of black man at hands of police

    The U.S. President says the federal government will launch a civil rights investigation into the death of a New York man who was choked to death by a police officer.
