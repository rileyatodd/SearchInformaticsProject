NHL gets it right with Slava Voynov's domestic violence suspension

by Allan Muir
0 0 0
0 0 0
A preliminary hearing in the domestic violence case against Slava Voynov opened on Monday, allowing details about the alleged assault to emerge. If proved true, Voynov will likely face prison time and the end of his NHL career.

According to Redondo Beach (Calif.) police, the Kings defenseman punched, kicked and choked his wife, shoving her into a TV in the aftermath of an earlier dispute. The resulting injury above her eye required eight stitches to close and left the couple's bedroom covered in blood.

Nathan Fenno of the Los Angeles Times is covering the hearing:

Voynov has remained impassive during the hearing, head slightly bowed as an interpreter whispers in his left ear.
