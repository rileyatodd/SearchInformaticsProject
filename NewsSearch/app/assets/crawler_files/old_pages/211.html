Judge orders Kings' Voynov to stand trial on count of spousal abuse

TORRANCE - Kings defenseman Slava Voynov was ordered to stand trial on a felony count of spousal abuse by a Los Angeles Superior Court judge late Monday morning.

Voynov faces a count of corporal injury to spouse with great bodily injury and, if convicted, faces a maximum sentence of nine years in state prison. Voynov, a Russian citizen, also might face deportation.

Judge Hector M. Guzman ordered Voynov to stand trial after he listened to lengthy, detailed testimony from Officer Gregory Wiist of the Redondo Beach Police Department. Wiist testified that he interviewed Voynov’s wife, Marta Varlamova, after an incident at the couple’s Redondo Beach home on Oct. 19.

One of Voynov’s attorney’s Craig Renetzky, made two requests to the court: that the case be dismissed or that the charge be reduced to a misdemeanor. Both were denied by Guzman, who ordered Voynov to return to court Dec. 29 for arraignment.

According to testimony, Varlamova told Wiist that she had been punched in the face, choked, kicked "all over her body’" and pushed into the corner of a wall-mounted television by Voynov. Wiist said her face was bloodied and swollen and she was in need of eight stitches to close a cut.

"She was crying and sobbing," Wiist said. "I saw tears streaming down her face. She was an emotional wreck."

Under questioning from Deputy District Attorney Frank Dunnick, Wiist gave a detailed narrative about how he encountered Varlamova and Voynov, what he saw and what he was told.

Wiist said he received a call about a "family fight" on the street on which Voynov and Varlamova live. Approximately one hour later, Wiist was called to Little Company of Mary Medical Center in Torrance, where he saw and spoke with Varlamova for the first time.

In addition to the cut near her left eye, which left blood "streaming," Varlamova also had "numerous red marks around her neck area," Wiist testified.

"Marta told me that she was involved in a physical altercation with her husband," Wiist said. "She idenfified her husband as Slava Voynov, number 26 on the Los Angeles Kings hockey team."

Wiist testified that Varlamova described an argument between her and Voynov at a Halloween party earlier that evening. Wiist said he was told it started after Voynov deliberately broke a pair of costume glasses that Varlamova had been wearing.

The argument moved outside, at which point Voynov "struck her in the face with his fist," Wiist said Varlamova told him.

Wiist said he was told the couple went back into the party and later went home together, at which point the argument escalated further. Wiist testified that Varlamova described being kicked, choked and repeatedly shoved to the ground, until finally she was shoved into the corner of a wall-mounted television and struck her face.

That’s how, according to Wiist, Varlamova suffered what he called a three-centimeter cut on her face.

"She asked him to stop," Wiist testified as he described his interview with Varlamova. "She was screaming. She indicated she was scared. When Mr. Voynov saw the blood coming out of her face, it wasn’t until then that it (the attack) stopped."
