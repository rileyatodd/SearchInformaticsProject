Sydney siege: What we do and don't know about hostage situation in Martin Place

Updated December 16, 2014 10:20:23

Photo: People run with their hands up from the Lindt Cafe, Martin Place in Sydney during a hostage standoff. (Getty Images: Joosep Martinson)
Map: Sydney 2000

Rumours are swirling around Sydney and across social media about the siege at a central Sydney cafe which was brought to an end when armed officers stormed the building early on Tuesday. Here's what we do and don't know.

Follow our live blog for rolling updates on the situation.

What we do know

  • Iranian cleric Man Haron Monis took 17 people hostage in the Lindt Chocolate Cafe at Martin Place in central Sydney about 9:45am AEDT on Monday.
  • Two hostages and Monis died as the siege ended in gunfire about 2:00am AEDT on Tuesday.
  • The two hostages killed were 38-year-old barrister Katrina Dawson and the cafe's manager, Tori Johnson, aged 34.
  • Four other people required hospital treatment for non-life-threatening injuries, including a police officer.
  • Police stormed the building shortly after 2:00am; a number of gunshots and yelling were heard from inside the cafe.
  • Monis was on bail for a string of violent offences, including being an accessory to the murder of his ex-wife.
  • Monis' former lawyer, Manny Conditsis, said Monis was an isolated figure likely to be acting alone.
  • Monis had "a long history of violent crime, infatuation with extremism and mental instability", according to Prime Minister Tony Abbott.
  • Police had been in direct contact with Monis during the siege.
  • A number of hostages had been seen with their hands pressed up against the glass of the cafe windows.
  • Five people fled the cafe on Monday afternoon, having escaped from Monis.
  • A black flag with an Islamic creed written on it in Arabic was displayed in the windows early in the siege, but the flag was not the one used by Islamic State.
  • Hostages contacted a number of media outlets, including the ABC, during the siege. Police asked the media not to reveal the contents of the calls.

What we don't know

  • The exact circumstances of the final police raid and how the two hostages died.
  • The complete details of the motivation or aims of Monis.
  • Exactly what weapons or explosives Monis may have had access to.
