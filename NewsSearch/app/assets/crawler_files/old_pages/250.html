E-Cigarette use among teens is rising

December 15, 2014 by Alessia Ruissen in Health & Medical · 1 Comment

According to two separate studies, Electronic cigarettes (E-Cigarettes) are attracting more teenagers, while the use of traditional cigarettes and tobacco is falling.

One study was conducted by the University of Hawaii Cancer Center on teens and students in Hawaii, while the other study was conducted in Connecticut. Researchers found similar trends in both the studies, where more and more teenagers were opting for the E-Cigarettes over the traditional cigarettes.

The two studies found that in Connecticut 25 percent of teenagers use E-cigarettes while 12percent started using them this past month. Meanwhile, in Hawaii 29 percent of teenagers are using E-cigarettes while 18 percent have started using them this past month. Officials now fear that E-cigarettes could trap another generation in smoking. The researchers believe that the use of E-cigarettes has doubled yearly since 2009.

The studies show an increase in the use of E-cigarettes amongst teenagers, compared to the last study conducted by Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC). The CDC study had showed that 4.5 percent of high school students have used E-cigarettes in the earlier month, while 1.1 percent of middle school students had used them in that month.

Dr. Thomas Wills, the interim director of the UH Cancer Center’s Prevention and Control Program said, “You have to think carefully about the risks and benefits of using either tobacco or nicotine, which is known to be an addictive substance. A lot of teens think it is easy to quit smoking but it isn’t true. It’s hard for anybody to quit.”

According to the CDC and The Food and Drugs Administration (FDA), there were 11.7 billion dollars worth of E-Cigarettes sold in 2013 alone.

FDA has already asked E-cigarette manufacturers to register their product with the FDA and forbid its sale to teenagers under 18. According to the CDC, minors can’t purchase the device in 40 states. Despite that there are still almost 16 million underage teens that live in states that allow them to buy E-cigarettes legally.

The studies also show that many teenagers who have tried E-Cigarettes have not tried traditional cigarettes, alcohol or drugs. It also shows that most teenagers think E-Cigarettes are not as dangerous as traditional cigarettes. In fact the sale of traditional cigarettes amongst teenagers has reduced by half.
