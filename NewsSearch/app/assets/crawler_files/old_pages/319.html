A northern white rhino has died, leaving just five left in the world

Harry Readhead for Metro.co.ukMonday 15 Dec 2014 10:20 pm

Angalifu passed away. 5 northern white rhinos remain #RIP #EndExtinction Pls share condolences. Photo: Helene Hoffman pic.twitter.com/X0sEkWNJAF

— San Diego Zoo Safari (@sdzsafaripark) December 15, 2014

The death of a northern white rhino at San Diego Zoo Safari Park means there are now just five of the species left in the world.

Angalifu was 44 when he died of old age on Sunday, bringing his species one step closer to extinction.

‘Angalifu’s death is a tremendous loss to all of us, not only because he was well beloved here at the park but also because his death brings this wonderful species one step closer to extinction,’ safari park curator Randy Rieches told AP in a statement.

The remaining five of the species are all in captivity. One, an elderly female named Nola, is at San Diego Zoo Safari Park but was unable to breed with Angalifu while he was alive.

Sudan is the last remaining male, and is kept in Kenya alongside two females, Najin and Fatu. An elderly female is also at the Dvur Kralove Zoo in the Czech Republic.

Experts say Sudan is probably no longer capable of breeding, mean it is down to ‘artificial reproductive techniques’ to save the rhinos. If science can’t help, the species if doomed to extinction.

Like many other rhinoceros species, the northern white rhino population was decimated by poachers who sold their horns on the black market.
