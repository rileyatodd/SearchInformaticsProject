Suspect in Pennsylvania shooting is Iraq war veteran; 6 dead

Police searching for Pennsylvania man suspected of shooting and killing 6 on Monday
Suspect in Pennsylvania shooting is an Iraq War veteran

Officials in a Philadelphia suburb are looking for an Iraq war veteran they say is a suspect in a shooting rampage that left six of his relatives dead Monday.

Former Marine Corps Sgt. Bradley William Stone, 35, of Pennsburg, Pa., is being sought in the shootings in Lansdale, Lower Salford Township and Souderton.

Montgomery County law enforcement officials said they considered Stone armed and dangerous, and that he often used a cane or a walker and might be wearing military fatigues. As of Monday afternoon, local media reported that SWAT teams had surrounded a home in Pennsburg, and a news conference by public safety officials was delayed.

All the victims have a familial relationship to Stone, police said, but they did not identify them. 

The Marine Corps confirmed Stone’s service, telling the Los Angeles Times that he enlisted in 2002 and left as a reservist in 2008. He served in Iraq from April 17, 2008, to July 2, 2008, the only deployment on his record, according to a spokeswoman for the Marines.

He earned an Iraq Campaign Medal -- a merit indicating service in Iraq -- in addition to a Global War on Terrorism Service Medal and other commendations. His job included monitoring weather conditions for artillery. Further details on his deployment or deployments were not immediately available.

A Facebook page under Stone’s name said he had a son and showed a photo of him kissing the bride at a wedding. Among favorite quotes listed on the Facebook page: “If you [expletive] with me, I'll kill you all,” and "Be polite, be professional, but have a plan to kill everyone you meet."

Several Pennsylvania law enforcement agencies on Monday morning said they surrounded a residence in Souderton, but Towamencin Township Police Chief Tim Dickinson told Philly.com the person inside was not the suspected gunman.

At least one shooting occurred just before 4:30 a.m., said a spokeswoman for the Lower Salford Township Police Department.

The Souderton Area School District issued a “shelter in place” alert Monday morning and said all of its schools are secure.

Montgomery County is a suburb north of Philadelphia.
