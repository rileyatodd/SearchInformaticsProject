    A pro-Main Street policy

    The Dec. 12 front-page article “Democrats’ Warren wing sends message” referred to Sen. Elizabeth Warren (D-Mass.) and her supporters as “the populist anti-Wall Street faction” of the congressional Democrats. This characterization is misleading and untrue. Objection to a rider that would allow banks to trade risky derivatives, default swaps and commodities with the full backing of the Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. is not “anti-Wall Street.”

    For those with a short memory, derivative trading was a main cause of the 2008 financial meltdown and subsequent bailouts. The Dodd-Frank bill was enacted to prevent a recurrence of government bailouts caused by this type of trading.

    If banks want to engage in this type of trading and activity, they should do so without the backing of the U.S. government. This is not anti-Wall Street; it is pro-Main Street. The butcher, the baker and the candlestick-maker do not have powerful lobbies as the bankers do. All they have are Ms. Warren and her ilk to represent and protect them in the halls of power.

    Marc Lemberger, Silver Spring
