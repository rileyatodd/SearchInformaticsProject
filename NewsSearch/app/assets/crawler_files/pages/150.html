    Stephen Collins Confesses to Sexual Abuse Allegations: "I Did Something Terribly Wrong"

    By Lynsey Eidell
    3 hours ago

    More than two months after allegations of child molestation surfaced in October, actor Stephen Collins—who played pastor Eric Camden on 7th Heaven—has released a lengthy confession and apology, according to People. "I did something terribly wrong that I deeply regret," the actor tells the magazine.

    Trouble first surfaced for the 67-year-old actor when TMZ released audio from a 2012 therapy session secretly recorded by his wife at the time, Faye Grant. During the tapes, Collins allegedly admitted to several instances of inappropriate sexual conduct with young girls. Following the tapes' release, Collins was cut from Ted 2 and reruns of 7th Heaven were pulled from the air.

    Now the actor is speaking out for the first time—and what he has to say is shocking, to say the least.

    Collins told People: "Forty years ago, I did something terribly wrong that I deeply regret. I have been working to atone for it ever since. I've decided to address these issues publicly because two months ago, various news organizations published a recording made by my then-wife, Faye Grant, during a confidential marriage therapy session in January 2012. This session was recorded without the therapist's or my knowledge or consent. On the recording, I described events that took place 20, 32, and 40 years ago. The publication of the recording has resulted in assumptions and innuendos about what I did that go far beyond what actually occurred. As difficult as this is, I want people to know the truth."

    The truth, according to Collins' statement to People, is that there were three victims between the years of 1973 and 1994, but Collins has "not had an impulse to act out in any such way" since then.

    As for the victims, Collins has made one personal apology. "I did have an opportunity to do so with one of the women, 15 years later. I apologized and she was extraordinarily gracious," Collins told People. "But after I learned in the course of my treatment that my being direct about such matters could actually make things worse for them by opening old wounds, I have not approached the other two women, one of whom is now in her 50s and the other in her 30s."

    Katie Couric will conduct in-depth interview with Collins on his confession, to air Friday on 20/20.
