      'The Voice' Season 7 winner is....; 'The Simpsons' 25th birthday; Stephen Collins sexual abuse confession: TV Talk

      The Voice - Season 7
      "The Voice" announced its Season 7 winner Tuesday night, which was good news for one of the four finalists, from left: Craig Wayne Boyd, Chris Jamison, Damien Lawson, and Matt McAndrew. That's Carson Daly on the right, as if you didn't know. (NBC)
      Print
      Kristi Turnquist | kturnquist@oregonian.com By Kristi Turnquist | kturnquist@oregonian.com The Oregonian
      Email the author | Follow on Twitter
      on December 17, 2014 at 7:06 AM, updated December 17, 2014 at 7:36 AM
      View/Post Comments

      Today's TV Talk includes the Season 7 winner of NBC's "The Voice"; a reminder that "The Simpsons" premiered 25 years ago today; and "7th Heaven" star Stephen Collins opening up about allegations that he sexually abused underage girls.

      "The Voice" winner is...: Portland's Taylor John Williams got a bit more TV time in some group musical numbers, but the final four competing in Tuesday night's Season 7 finale of "The Voice" were Craig Wayne Boyd; Matt McAndrews; Chris Jamison; and Damien. When the two-hour finale was over, it all came down to a few minutes, as the winner was announced -- it was Craig Wayne Boyd, the country singer and member of celebrity coach Blake Shelton's team. 

      Today.com has a rundown of the finals. And though there were lots of mentions that Boyd was an underdog, the Washington Post's Emily Yahr makes the totally accurate argument that country singers are far from underdogs when it comes to reality competition shows. For more details, check out Entertainment Weekly's recap of the finale, which included such highlights as Bruno Mars performing with gold curlers in his hair.

      "The Simpsons" celebrates the big 2-5: The animated series-turned-pop-culture blockbuster created by Portland's own Matt Groening premiered 25 years ago today. Here's a look at 25 things you may not know about Homer, Bart, Marge, and the gang. Sample: "Groening himself provides the pacifier-sucking sound for baby Maggie."

      Stephen Collins opens up about sexual abuse allegations: The actor best known for playing a pastor and devoted husband and father in the TV series "7th Heaven" has released a statement to People magazine in which, as the People website says, Collins "admits to inappropriate sexual contact with three female minors." The scandal surrounding Collins began when a recording of the actor apparently confessing to molesting children was publicized by TMZ.

      Do you have questions, rants or raves about what you're watching? Join Kristi Turnquist for TV Talk Live, our weekly live chat here at Oregonlive.com/tv, every Thursday at noon.

      -- Kristi Turnquist
