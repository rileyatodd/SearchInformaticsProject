JI joins countrywide mourning of Peshawar school tragedy

  • INP
  • December 17, 2014, 8:55 pm
  • Comment
Tweet

LAHORE – The Jamaat-e-Islami (JI) joined the countrywide mourning for the students and teachers martyred in the Peshawar militant attack with a Ghaibana Namaz-e-Janaza (funeral prayer in absentia) held outside Mansoora.


JI Secretary General Liaqat Baloch, who led the funeral prayer, vehemently condemned the terrorist attack and demanded that the hidden hand behind the attack be exposed and the nation informed of that. He said that all the forces against Islam and Pakistan had ganged up to crush the Muslims.


He said that the US and its allies had disturbed the world peace and the Muslims were being targeted in Iraq, Syria and Afghanistan. He said that the world at large was in search of peace and it was imperative that the world powers reviewed their policies and abandoned their biases against Islam and the Muslims.


He said that the situation also demanded that the national leadership sat together, to meet the challenges. Liaqat said that Pakistan had suffered highest loss of life and property of the 9/11 fallout as the US and India were commanding terrorist activities inside Pakistan from Afghanistan.


He said the entire Muslim world was in grief over the Peshawar tragedy. The JI leader said that the so called war on terror had cost lives of more than 60,000 people besides financial losses of billions of dollars. He added that Pakistan country was confronted with several crises and had been virtually turned into the centre of terrorism.


Speaking on the occasion, JI deputy chief Hafiz Muhammad Idrees said that after the 9/11, Pakistan had been the target of terrorism and subversive activities, but no government had thought of pulling out of the US war for freeing the masses from terrorists’ grip.


He said that the Black Water was roaming about freely in Pakistan. JI deputy chief and ex-lawmakers Mian Muhammad Aslam, said that the Peshawar terrorist attack indicated that the security agencies were not discharging their duties properly.


He said that the Peshawar tragedy was a well planned conspiracy in which US and India were involved. Maulana Abdul Maalik, Dr Farid Ahmed Piracha and Punjab chief Dr Syed Waseem Akhtar also spoke on the occasion.


In a statement here, Islamic Education Organization Pakistan Director General Misbahul Huda Siddiqi expressed deep grief over the heavy loss of life in the terrorist attack at the Army Public School. He said that the tragedy had plunged the entire Muslim world in grief. He appealed to the educational institutions to pray for the bereaved families and also to keep a vigilant eye on the miscreants around them.
