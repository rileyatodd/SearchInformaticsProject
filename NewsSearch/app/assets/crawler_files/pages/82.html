SAN JOSE -- Police are investigating San Francisco 49ers defensive lineman Ray McDonald after a woman claimed he sexually assaulted her at his home earlier this week, authorities said.

A search warrant was served Tuesday at McDonald's Silver Creek valley residence, the same day police got a call from a local hospital to report a possible sexual-assault victim. Police say the woman told investigators that she had been assaulted "a day prior."

"The preliminary investigation revealed the alleged suspect was Ray McDonald," San Jose police spokeswoman Sgt. Heather Randol said in an email.

McDonald has not been arrested, and the investigation is ongoing, Randol said, adding that once it's complete, it will be forwarded to the Santa Clara County District Attorney's Office for review.

Ray McDonald, defensive tackle of the San Francisco 49ers, at the Niners training camp facility in Santa Clara, Calif., on Wednesday, July 31, 2013. (Gary Reyes/Bay Area News Group) (Gary Reyes)

The allegation marks another serious run-in with the law for McDonald, who had just been investigated for domestic violence stemming from an Aug. 31 fight with his pregnant fiancee. The District Attorney's Office announced last month that it would not file any charges, citing insufficient evidence, and said it appeared the fiancee initiated the physical contact that led to his initial arrest.

That case was complicated by McDonald calling a San Jose police sergeant, who moonlighted for the team security's detail, to his home prior to officers arriving for a separate 911 call, which spurred a parallel investigation and evoked national criticism about a potentially conflicting relationship between the team and police. Prosecutors ultimately decided it was ancillary to the case, but police brass indefinitely suspended its officers from working for the team and launched an internal review.
