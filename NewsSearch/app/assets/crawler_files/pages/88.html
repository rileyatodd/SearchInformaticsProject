San Francisco 49ers' Ray McDonald Suspect in Alleged Sexual Assault: San Jose Police

By Damian Trujillo and Lisa Fernandez

    Wednesday, Dec 17, 2014 • Updated at 10:47 AM PST

    San Francisco 49ers defensive end Ray McDonald is being investigated by San Jose police for an alleged sexual assault, authorities confirmed Wednesday morning.

    Police executed a search warrant at McDonald's San Jose home on Tuesday, NBC Bay Area has learned.

    • Video49er Ray McDonald's Parents Criticize Police in Son's Arrest

    In a statement, SJPD Sgt. Heather Randol said that police were called to an area hospital at 10:43 a.m. Tuesday morning regarding a "possible sexual assault victim" who was receiving treatment. Officers spoke to the woman, and she told them she was "possibly assaulted" on Monday.

    The "preliminary investigation revealed the alleged suspect was Ray McDonald," Randol said.

    San Francisco 49ers 2014-15 Season Photos

    The San Jose Police Department Sexual Assaults Investigations Unit was notified and responded to conduct follow-up investigation.  Based on preliminary investigation, detectives secured a search warrant at McDonald's home.

    Reached by phone in Florida on Wednesday, McDonald's mother, Labrina McDonald, said she didn't yet know the full story but insisted it was a "bunch of mess. There's  no truth to it at all."

    • "7th Heaven" Star Confesses to Sex Abuse of Young Girls

    McDonald, 30, was arrested in August after his fiancee at the time accused him of choking her. In November, the Santa Clara County District Attorney decided there was not enough evidence to charge him.

    No arrests have been made.

    When asked for a comment, an NFL spokesman said, "We are looking into it," although early indications were he'd be allowed to play Saturday against the Chargers.
